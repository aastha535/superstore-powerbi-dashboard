##Project Background

Retail businesses generate large volumes of transactional data, but without proper analysis, valuable insights remain hidden. This case study focuses on analyzing Superstore sales data to understand performance trends, customer behavior, and operational efficiency using Power BI.

Business Problem
The objective was to answer key business questions such as:

##How are sales and profits trending over time?

Which product categories and sub-categories drive the most revenue?

Which regions and cities perform best?

How do shipping modes and payment methods impact sales?

Data Understanding

The dataset contains information on:

Orders and sales values

Profit and shiping days
Product categories and sub-categories

Customer segments

Geographic details (region and city)

Payment and shipping modes

Approach

Imported and cleaned the dataset in Power BI

Created calculated measures for sales, profit, orders, and averages

Designed KPI cards to highlight key business metrics
Built interactive visuals including line charts, bar charts, and donut charts

Added slicers for year and region to allow dynamic filtering

##Key Insights

Sales and profit increase steadily in the later months of the year

A small number of categories contribute a large share of revenue

Shipping performance varies across modes, impacting delivery timelines

Regional analysis reveals opportunities for targeted growth

Business Value

The dashboard helps stakeholders:

Monitor performance at a glance

Identify high-performing products and regions

Optimize shipping and operational strategies

Make data-driven decisions based on trends and comparisons

#Conclusion
This Power BI project highlights the importance of data visualization in retail analytics. By converting complex datasets into an intuitive dashboard, the analysis enables better understanding of business performance and supports informed decision-making.
